Dunstaffnage Caste

Dunstaffnage is a thirteenth century castle near Oban, Argyllshire. Formerly a MacDougall stringhold, it was seized by Robert the Bruce in 1309 and entrusted to Campbell caln on behalf of the crown.

A bank of wil flowers with castle grazing, a laugh and castle beyond

During 1830s Nicholl concentrated on illustrating the wild scenary of the Ireland especially the Antrim coast. This work is best known for his highly original views of Irish coastal towns viewed through banks of wild flowers.

Landscape with Procris and Cephalus, after Cloude

This painting tells the story from Ovid, where Cephalus has mistakenly speared his wife Procris and looks upon her in horror.

Classical Pillar, Alexandria, Egypt

Nicholl sailed to Ceylon for a position as a art master at the school during August 1846. This painting was made either on the outwards or on the return journey.

Dun Laoghaire Harbour and Dublin Bay

Nicholl's technique had a principle influence of Turner. In this painitng, he used a technique of "sgraffito" i.e. scraping to highlight the grass in the foreground and to contrast the effect against the background.

Cattle on a Riverbank

This painting depicts Nicholl's interest in topographical art, combined with his technique in botanical illustration which became popular in the eighteenth century
