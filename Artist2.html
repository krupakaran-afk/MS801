Gallery

The Hackney Car

This piece heralds the emergence of Yeats' genuine style, which is a type of Impressionism. Jack used paint to depict the imagery and sometimes mystical characteristics of Irish folklore.

Dublin Evening

Yeats' uses expressive impasto and quickly applied paint to create a romantic, highly personal painting that captures his childhood memories of light and scenery.

Queen Maeve Walked upon this Strand

This is a late artwork by Yeats. Throughout the latter twenty years of his life, he frequently returned to Celtic themes.

Sea Wind - 1950

This rocky Irish seaside view's vivid colors and flashing, artistic approach is typical of his late style.

Men of Destiny

Yeats examines the idea of figures leaving their regular jobs to fight for independence by drawing on his visual experience of Sligo fisherman disembarking at Rosses Point and tying the boats.

The Riverside(Long Ago)

This picture is based on memories of Yeats' upbringing in Sligo, which have been reinterpreted to reflect universal themes of human existence.
